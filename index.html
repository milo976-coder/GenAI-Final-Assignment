Exploring the complexities of Clinical Breakpoints and Antimicrobial Resistance Surveillance

Context

What are Clinical Breakpoints?

  • The Cutoff Line: A number that determines exactly how much of an antibiotic is needed to successfully stop a bacterial infection.
  • The Susceptible vs. Resistant Label: It categorizes bacteria based on drug safety limits.
    • If a safe, normal dose stops the bacteria, it is labelled "Susceptible" (the drug works).
    • If it requires a dose higher than a human can safely handle, it is labelled "Resistant" (the drug fails).

THE PROBLEM

Breakpoint Changes & AMR Surveillance

  • Breakpoint Drift - Artificial shifts in interpretive data that confound long-term trend tracking.
  • Perceived vs. Real Resistance - Stricter definitions can trigger a false appearance of microbial evolution.
  • Skewed Global Trends - Failure to retroactively adjust historical data severely distorts burden estimates.
  • Incompatible Data - Divergent standards between regional bodies ([CLSI] vs [EUCAST]) prevent international comparison.
  • Software Update Lag - Regulatory delays in commercial testing systems leave labs using obsolete criteria.
